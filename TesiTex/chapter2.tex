\section{Big Data and Predictive Analysis}

The concept of Big Data is related to the Internet of Things: IoT devices are capable of collecting terabytes of data in short time, therefore is evident the need for a software capable of interpreting and analyzing data at a very high rate in a short period.

Nonetheless, IoT devices are not the only players capable of generating profiling data at such an incredibly high rate;  the modern Web represents another essential data source in this paradigm: social networks, for example, are capable of producing an endless stream of data describing user preferences and behaviors at any time.

This vast volume of data available to analyzers is behind the term Big Data which can be taught as a massive dataset, growing at a very high velocity in numerous formats from a variety of different origins.

To obtain business value from this always moving creature companies necessitate algorithms and solutions fitted to efficiently process, validate and give credibily to this amount of information, solutions beyond traditional systems currently used for managing and storing information, with high power and high calculation inclination capable of sorting data in a structured and meaningful manner to detect relationships and patterns.

This new approach to data management differs from what companies used to do in the past when priorities were bound to an IT level governance only and they were solely accessed by a restricted set of users. 

It also becomes fundamental for a company to correctly identify new sources of big data when they become available on the market and incorporate them into the above-mentioned platform of data management following a constant continuous improvement logic.
